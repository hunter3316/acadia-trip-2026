# Acadia National Park - Trip Research & Constraints
*Synthesized from nps.gov/acad for June 2026 Trip Planning*

This document outlines all the critical information and constraints gathered directly from the National Park Service official website, tailored to the family's 6-person, 1-dog RV trip for June 2026.

## 1. RV Camping & Hookup Constraints
There is a major trade-off regarding staying inside the National Park with an RV that requires hookups.

*   **Schoodic Woods Campground:** 
    *   **Hookups:** YES! Offers RV sites with **Electric only** ($36/night) and **Electric & Water** ($40/night). No sewer/septic available.
    *   **Location Catch:** This campground is on the Schoodic Peninsula (the mainland section of the park). It is about a **1 hour and 15 minute drive** from Bar Harbor and the main Mount Desert Island (MDI) area of the park. 
*   **Blackwoods & Seawall Campgrounds (Main Park/MDI):**
    *   **Hookups:** NO. These campgrounds only offer dry camping (tent, camper, and motorhome sites for $30/night). There are no water or electric hookups. Showers are not available on-site (fee-operated showers are in nearby towns).
*   **Recommendation:** If you absolutely require water/electric hookups but want to be near the main park attractions (Mount Desert Island), you will likely need to book a **private campground** located on MDI outside of the official NPS park boundaries.

## 2. Campground Reservation Timeline
*   All NPS campsites must be reserved in advance via **Recreation.gov**. There are no in-person or same-day walk-up campground office reservations.
*   **Critical Date:** 90% of campsites are released **6 months in advance** on the 1st of the month at 10:00 AM EST. For a June trip, you need to book on **December 1st at 10:00 AM EST**.
*   The remaining 10% of sites are released 14 days ahead of the desired arrival date.

## 3. Vehicle Reservations (Cadillac Summit Road)
*   **Reservations:** You must purchase a $6 vehicle reservation online (Recreation.gov) to drive up the Cadillac Summit Road from mid-May to mid-October. They are not sold at the park.
*   **CRITICAL CONSTRAINT - NO RVs/TRAILERS:** Vehicles over 21 feet long, recreation vehicles, and **trailers are prohibited** on Cadillac Summit Road. You will need to unhitch the trailer at your campsite and drive just the Chevy Suburban up the mountain. 

## 4. Dog / Pet Policies (Bark Ranger)
Acadia is broadly pet-friendly, but there are strict limitations for June:
*   **Where Dogs ARE Allowed:**
    *   100 miles of hiking trails and 45 miles of carriage roads.
    *   Inside Blackwoods, Seawall, and Schoodic Woods campgrounds.
*   **Where Dogs are STRICTLY PROHIBITED:**
    *   **Sand Beach:** Closed to pets from June 15 to Sept 8.
    *   **Echo Lake:** Closed to pets from May 15 to Sept 15.
    *   **Most Lakes:** Public water supplies; pets and people may not swim in them.
    *   **Public Buildings & Ranger-led programs.**
    *   **Steep/Specific Trails:** Precipice, Beehive, Ladder Trail, Beech Cliffs, Perpendicular, and Jordan Cliffs (these involve iron rungs/ladders and are dangerous for dogs).
*   *Dogs must be on a leash (6 ft or less) at all times.*

## 5. Entrance Passes & Fees
*   **Standard Entrance Pass:** Costs **$35.00** for a private vehicle. This pass covers the Chevy Suburban and everyone inside it for the duration of the pass (typically 7 days).
*   All vehicles in campgrounds must prominently display their National Park Entrance Pass on the windshield.
*   The park encourages planning to go cashless (credit/debit only at visitor centers and entrance stations).

## 6. Photography & Wildlife Viewing
*   **Puffins:** June is peak nesting season! You can't see them directly from the park's coast, but you can book a Puffin and Lighthouse boat tour out of Bar Harbor (e.g., to Petit Manan or Machias Seal Island).
*   **Whales:** June is fantastic for whale watching. Humpback, minke, and finback whales are common. Whale watching cruises leave directly from Bar Harbor.
*   **Moose:** Very rare on Mount Desert Island. Moose prefer inland, less populated areas of northern Maine. It is very unlikely you will see them in Acadia.
*   **Ocean Cliff Photography:**
    *   **Ocean Path & Ocean Drive:** This is the iconic coastal stretch.
    *   **Otter Cliffs:** 110-foot high granite cliffs. Incredible for sunrise photography with sea mist.
    *   **Monument Cove & Boulder Beach:** Excellent foregrounds for landscape shots.
    *   **Schooner Head Overlook & Thunder Hole:** Classic dramatic wave crashing and rock formations.
    *   **Bass Harbor Head Light Station:** Iconic sea-cliff lighthouse, perfect for sunset (parking fills up extremely fast).

## 7. Tide Pooling
The best time for tide pooling is 1.5 hours before and after low tide. Be sure to check tide charts daily and wear grippy, closed-toe shoes.
*   **Wonderland Trail:** 1.3-mile easy trail leading to some of the best and most accessible tide pools on the island (sea stars, crabs, snails).
*   **Ship Harbor Trail:** Located right next to Wonderland, offers deeper pools.
*   **Bar Island:** Accessible only at low tide via a gravel bar from Bar Harbor. Great for beachcombing.

## 8. Oceanside RV Camping Options (With Hookups)
Since you want ocean views, coastline access, and RV hookups, you should look at private RV resorts on Mount Desert Island, as the NPS campgrounds don't offer hookups on the coast.
*   **Bar Harbor / Oceanside KOA Holiday:** Located on the west side of MDI. Waterfront access, sweeping ocean views, and full hookups (50/30 amp).
*   **Mount Desert Narrows Camping Resort:** Panoramic ocean views, waterfront sites, full hookups.
*   **Narrows Too Camping Resort:** Located just off the island in Trenton, overlooking MDI with great waterfront RV sites.
*   **Bar Harbor Campground:** Scenic bluff with ocean views. Offers water/electric sites.
