## 🟢 GOOD TO DO — Packing & Prep

### 🎒 Packing Categories

- [ ] **RV & Campsite Essentials**
  - Hitch lock, leveling blocks, wheel chocks
  - Sewer hose + adapters (if not included)
  - Fresh water hose + pressure regulator
  - Extension cord (30A or 50A to 30A adapter)
  - Outdoor mat / rug (for under the awning)

- [ ] **Kitchen & Food**
  - Cooler + ice for day trips away from RV
  - Reusable plates, cups, utensils
  - Coffee maker or percolator
  - Pantry staples + first-night easy dinner
  - Trash bags, paper towels, aluminum foil
  - Grill tools (charcoal/propane for campfire grill)

- [ ] **Clothing & Personal**
  - Layers! June in Maine: 50°F mornings → 75°F afternoons
  - Rain jacket / poncho (Maine drizzle is common)
  - Grippy shoes / water shoes for tide pooling
  - Swimsuits (Sand Beach is cold but swimmable!)
  - DEET insect repellent — black flies peak in June
  - Sunscreen SPF 30+

- [ ] **Dog Supplies**
  - 6-ft leash (required in all NPS areas)
  - Water bowl + portable water bottle
  - Poop bags
  - Dog bed / crate for the RV
  - Vet records / vaccination proof (some campgrounds ask)

- [ ] **Photography & Electronics**
  - Camera + wide angle lens for Cadillac sunrise
  - Tripod (for sunrise / astrophotography)
  - Extra batteries + memory cards
  - Phone chargers + USB power bank
  - Binoculars (for puffins, whales, peregrine falcons)

- [ ] **Safety & First Aid**
  - Basic first aid kit
  - Flashlights / headlamps
  - Fire extinguisher (check RV's built-in)
  - Roadside emergency kit for the Suburban
  - Maps / offline GPS (cell signal is unreliable in parts of MDI)

- [ ] **Fun & Entertainment**
  - Board games / card games for rainy evenings
  - Books / Kindle
  - Fishing gear (Maine saltwater license if interested)
  - Star chart / night sky app for astrophotography nights
  - Journal / sketchbook for the 13-year-old
