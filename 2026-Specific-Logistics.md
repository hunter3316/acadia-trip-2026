## 1. Important 2026 Dates & Events

*   **Acadia Birding Festival (May 28 - May 31, 2026):** Technically ends right before the June 6 arrival window. The *advantage* of this is that the massive crowds of birders will have just left the island, leaving the trails much less congested for your two photographers, while all the pelagic/breeding birds (like the Puffins, Warblers, and Loons) will still be present and highly active.
*   **Puffin Lighthouse Cruises:** Operated by Bar Harbor Whale Watch Co. and others. These cruises begin running in mid-May and operate daily through August. The 2026 June schedule will have daily departures (weather permitting). The cruise is 2.5 - 3 hours, which perfectly fits your dog's threshold for being comfortably left in the air-conditioned RV.

## 2. Solar Targets for Photographers (Early June 2026)

*   **Cadillac Mountain Sunrise:** In early June, sunrise occurs very early in Maine, generally around **4:50 AM to 5:00 AM**. To secure the "first light in the US" advantage and the critical blue hour, the photographers need to be passing the Cadillac Summit vehicle checkpoint by **4:00 AM**.
*   **Coastal Sunset:** Sunset occurs between **8:15 PM and 8:25 PM**. The golden hour will stretch perfectly over your campsite dinners if you secure the Bar Harbor/Oceanside KOA (west-facing) or when doing astrophotography out on the Schoodic Peninsula at Raven's Nest.

## 3. Tide Prediction Reference (June 6 Arrival)

Tides shift by about 50 minutes each day. On the opening weekend of your trip (June 6/7):
*   **Low Tide:** ~8:30 AM to 9:30 AM.
*   **High Tide:** ~2:45 PM to 3:30 PM.

**Strategic Advantage:** A morning low tide means the family can hit the **Wonderland Trail** or **Bar Island** land bridge immediately after breakfast to explore the tide pools, leaving the very hot mid-day for shaded hikes or relaxing at the RV, and preserving the late afternoon for photography.
