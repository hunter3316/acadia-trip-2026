🏔️ Top Things To Do in Acadia

Curated picks for a group of 6 with a 13-year-old and a golden retriever

⭐ Must Do#1

Cadillac Mountain Sunrise

The highest peak on the US Atlantic coast (1,530 ft). Be among the first people in America to see the sunrise — arrive by 4:00 AM to claim a spot. The summit panorama of Frenchman Bay, the Porcupine Islands, and the park's pink granite is unforgettable.

Requires a $6 vehicle reservation (no RVs/trailers — unhitch the Suburban). Reserve on Recreation.gov.

🚗 Drive up 🕓 ~4:00 AM arrival 🐕 No dogs at summit 🅿️ Reservation required
🌊 Dramatic#2

Thunder Hole & Ocean Path

A 2-mile paved coastal path leading to Thunder Hole — a natural slot where waves crash with a booming sound. Best at high tide (± 3 ft). The pink granite cliffs along Ocean Path are stunning for photography.

🚶 Easy walk 🐕 Dog friendly 📷 Photo gold 🌊 Best at high tide
🍞 Iconic#3

Jordan Pond & Popovers

Crystal-clear lake with The Bubble mountains reflected in it. The Jordan Pond House is famous for serving warm popovers with jam on the lawn — a beloved Acadia tradition since the 1890s. The 3.3-mile loop trail around the pond is flat and family-friendly.

🚶 Easy loop (3.3 mi) 🐕 Dog friendly 🍽️ Lunch stop 📷 Iconic views
🪜 Adventure#4

Beehive & Precipice Ladder Trails

Acadia's legendary iron-rung cliff trails! The Beehive Trail (1.5 mi loop) and Precipice Trail (1.6 mi) feature hand-and-foot climbing on iron rungs, ladders, and narrow granite ledges bolted into the cliff face — not for the faint of heart! The Beehive rewards with jaw-dropping views of Sand Beach and the coastline.

⚠️ Note: The Precipice Trail is typically closed mid-March through mid-August for peregrine falcon nesting — likely closed during our trip (June 8–12). The Beehive should be open and is the better option. Never hike in wet/rainy conditions — iron rungs get extremely slippery.

🪜 Iron rungs & ladders 🥾 Strenuous / Exposed 🐕 No dogs ⚠️ Heights required
🏖️ Beach Day#5

Sand Beach

One of the only sandy beaches in the park, nestled between towering granite headlands. Water is cold (~55°F) but refreshing. A great spot for the 13-year-old to explore! Also the trailhead for the Great Head Trail and the Beehive.

🏊 Swimming ⚠️ No dogs June 15 – Sept 8 🚶 Trailhead for Beehive
🚴 Family Fun#6

Carriage Roads & Stone Bridges

45 miles of motor-free crushed gravel roads built by John D. Rockefeller Jr. between 1913 and 1940. Perfect for biking, walking, or even booking a horse-drawn carriage ride. The 17 hand-cut granite bridges are architectural marvels — Hemlock Bridge with its waterfall is a favorite. Dog friendly!

🚲 Rent bikes locally 🐕 Dog friendly 👶 Kid friendly 🌉 17 stone bridges
🌅 Sunset#7

Bass Harbor Head Lighthouse

Maine's most iconic lighthouse, perched on dramatic sea cliffs on the "quiet side" of Mount Desert Island. Built in 1858 and still an active Coast Guard station. Parking fills up fast — go at sunrise or mid-day to avoid the sunset crowd.

🅿️ Very limited parking 🌅 Go early! 📷 Iconic shot 🐕 Dog on leash
🌊 Unique#8

Bar Island at Low Tide

A gravel bar emerges at low tide, walkable straight from downtown Bar Harbor to Bar Island — like magic. The bar is only exposed for ~2 hours around low tide, so time it carefully or you'll be stranded! Great for beachcombing and views of Frenchman Bay.

⏰ Tide-dependent 🐕 Dog friendly 🗺️ Walk from downtown ⚠️ Check tide chart!
🔬 Nature#9

Tide Pooling — Wonderland Trail

1.3-mile easy coastal trail leading to some of the best tide pools on the island. Find sea stars, hermit crabs, periwinkles, and sea anemones. Best 1.5 hrs before/after low tide. Wear grippy shoes on the wet granite!

🐕 Dog friendly ⏰ Check tide chart 👶 Kid favorite 🥿 Grippy shoes!
🥾 Hike#10

Gorham Mountain Trail

A family-friendly hike (1.8 miles round trip) with rewarding panoramic ocean views. A great alternative to the more intense Beehive — the summit is wide open with 360° views of the coast, Sand Beach, and Champlain Mountain. Perfect for a picnic.

🥾 Moderate 🐕 Dog friendly 🌊 Ocean views 👶 Kid friendly
🚗 Scenic Drive#11

Park Loop Road

The 27-mile scenic road connecting all of Acadia's major sites — Sand Beach, Thunder Hole, Otter Cliff, Jordan Pond, and Cadillac Mountain. A perfect rainy-day activity or orientation drive on your first day. Most of the road is one-way (counterclockwise), so plan accordingly.

🚗 27-mile loop 🅿️ Many pull-offs 🐕 Dog in car 🌧️ Great rainy-day option

🐦 Wildlife & Ocean Adventures

June is peak season — some of the best wildlife viewing of the year

Peak Season

Acadia Puffin Cruise

June is peak puffin nesting season! These colorful seabirds can't be seen directly from shore. For the absolutely closest views, we recommend the Acadia Puffin Cruise out of Steuben, ME. This dedicated 40' boat heads to Petit Manan Island (a National Wildlife Refuge) and can get much closer to the wildlife than the large catamarans. The cruise is ~2 hours, which is the perfect length for leaving the dog comfortably in the RV's A/C.

Book early — early June spots (right after the Acadia Birding Festival) are highly sought after by photographers.

Book Acadia Puffin Cruise → Alt: Bar Harbor Whales Alt: Acadian Boat Tours 🐧 Project Puffin — Audubon
🐧 Puffin Fun Facts & Viewing Tips

Quick Facts

  • Size: ~10 inches tall, ~1 lb — surprisingly small!
  • Diet: Sand lance, herring, hake — can carry 10–12 fish crosswise in its beak at once
  • Lifespan: 20–30 years; mate for life and return to the same burrow every year
  • Habitat: Underground burrows on grassy cliff-tops; spend 8 months at sea
  • Baby puffins are called "pufflings" 🥺

What to Bring

  • 🔭 Binoculars — essential; puffins are small and viewed from the water
  • 📷 Camera with 200mm+ zoom for decent photos
  • 🧥 Warm layers + windbreaker — ocean is cold even in June
  • 💊 Seasickness remedy — take Dramamine 30 min before departure

Conservation 🌿

Maine is the only US state where Atlantic Puffins breed. They were hunted to near-extinction by the 1800s, but Dr. Stephen Kress's Project Puffin (Audubon, est. 1973) transplanted chicks and used decoys to restore colonies. Today ~1,300 pairs nest across 5 Maine islands — one of the greatest seabird conservation success stories ever.

🐋 Whale Watching

June is fantastic for whales! Humpback, minke, and finback whales are commonly spotted on cruises out of Bar Harbor. These often combine with the Puffin tours for an incredible day on the water.

💡 Pro Tips & Mistakes to Avoid

Hard-won research so we don't learn the hard way

01

Book Cadillac Mountain NOW

A $6 timed vehicle reservation is required from late May to late October. Do NOT assume you can just drive up. 30% of tickets open 90 days out, 70% open 2 days before at 10 AM EST on Recreation.gov.

Book on Recreation.gov →
02

No RV/Trailer on Cadillac Summit Rd

Vehicles over 21 feet and any trailer are prohibited on the Cadillac Summit Road. Plan to unhitch the Suburban at the KOA and drive up separately.

03

Use the Island Explorer Shuttle

Parking in Bar Harbor and around the park is a nightmare in summer. The free Island Explorer bus stops at all major trailheads from Bar Harbor. Leave the Suburban at camp!

Explorer Bus Info →
04

Start Every Day Before 8 AM

Acadia gets extremely crowded in mid-June. Parking lots at Sand Beach, Thunder Hole, and Bass Harbor fill up by 9 AM on clear days. Early birds get the trails to themselves.

05

Bass Harbor: Go at Sunrise, Not Sunset

The Bass Harbor Lighthouse parking lot is tiny (12 cars!) and lines form 2+ hours before sunset. Visit at sunrise or mid-morning for stunning light and zero wait.

06

Dog Rules Are Strict — Know Them

Dogs are not allowed on Sand Beach (June 15–Sept 8), Echo Lake (May 15–Sept 15), or on the ladder trails (Precipice, Beehive, Jordan Cliffs). Always on a 6-ft leash. Most other trails are fine!

07

Check Falcon Trail Closures Daily

Peregrine falcons nest on cliff faces during June. Precipice Trail and sometimes Beehive are closed during nesting. Check the NPS app or visitor center boards each morning.

08

Black Flies Peak in June

June is peak black fly season in Maine — especially inland and in the woods. Bring DEET-based insect repellent. The ocean breezes at our KOA site will help keep them away at camp.

09

Thunder Hole Timing Matters

Thunder Hole is most dramatic at high tide with a ± 3 ft swell. A calm-day low tide produces a trickle. Check the tide chart and plan accordingly for the big boom!

10

Park Pass — $35 for 7 Days

One $35 America the Beautiful pass covers the Suburban and everyone inside for 7 consecutive days. Display it prominently on the windshield. No cash — credit/debit only at entrance stations.

NPS Fee Info →
11

Allow at Least 4 Full Days

The park is spread across MDI AND the Schoodic Peninsula. With 4 days at KOA, we're right at the minimum to hit the highlights. Every day counts — have a loose plan the night before.

12

Lupines & Late Spring Blooms

Early June is when Maine's famous lupine wildflowers bloom along roadsides (especially in the White Mountains / VT on the drive up). Stop for photos — they won't last!

🦌 Moose Photography Guide

A comprehensive field plan for your June 13–15 stay at Moose Creek RV Resort, Greenville, ME

Prime Season

Executive Summary

Mid-June is a fantastic moose photography window. Cows have very young calves, the animals are tied to water for sodium-rich aquatic vegetation, and you get nearly 17 hours of daylight. Your best bet is Lazy Tom Bog at first light, then Kokadjo village, with Prong Pond and Lily Bay as quiet alternatives.

Bottom line: Be in place before sunrise, prioritize water, and give cows with calves extra room.

📍 Priority Locations (Ranked)

All locations along the northbound Lily Bay Road corridor from the resort

1

🥇 Lazy Tom Bog Highest Yield

~35–40 min from resort · Dirt/gravel road past Kokadjo

The only location in the official Lily Bay park guide singled out explicitly for moose-watching. Moose feed on reeds at sunrise and dusk. The classic "moose-in-bog-with-mountain-backdrop" shot — Little Spencer Mountain reflected in the bog.

GPS: 45.69186, -69.43484
Access: Roadside/short walk. Small parking area near the bridge.
Best time: Dawn (less crowded) or sunset
Composition: Low angle from bridge shoulders — reeds, reflections, mountain backdrop
Driving Directions

Head toward Greenville, continue north on Lily Bay Road through Lily Bay State Park and on to Kokadjo. Go through Kokadjo (pavement ends), bear left where the sign points to Baxter State Park, go 1.1 miles, turn left at the sign for Spencer Pond Camps, then go 0.5 miles to the small bridge. Parking on either side.

2

🥈 Kokadjo & First Roach Pond Very High

~30 min from resort · End of paved road

Known locally as the "moose capital" — "where the pavement ends and moose outnumber people." Sits at the outlet of First Roach Pond with a public boat launch, water, mountains, and the Kokadjo Trading Post for a breakfast stop.

GPS: 45.67130, -69.44727 (Roach River launch area)
Access: Easy drive-up. Free parking at launch area.
Best time: Early morning for environmental portraits
Composition: Moose entering shallow water, pond + mountain backdrop, dawn mist
3

🥉 Prong Pond Trail Moderate

~15–20 min from resort · Short gravel approach

A quiet, secluded 1-mile trail (easy to moderate) through conserved landscape. Best for landscape-first environmental images. Views toward Big and Little Moose Mountains, Blue Ridge, and Elephant Mountain.

GPS: 45.541783, -69.5235393
Access: 7 mi north of Greenville + 0.35 mi gravel to large parking area
Trail: 1.0 mile one way, easy-moderate
Composition: Ridge overlook for long-lens shots, red-pine peninsula for moodier scenes
4

🏕️ Lily Bay State Park Best Backup

~15 min from resort · Paved, signed, formal parking

The easiest and most versatile backup. Excellent for foggy mornings, sunsets, and shoreline compositions even when moose don't cooperate. Day-use parking, beach, boat launches, and easy 2-mile shoreline trail.

GPS: 45.568853, -69.538450
Access: 9 mi north of Greenville, paved access
Hours: 9:00 AM – sunset (day use fee)
Composition: Dunn Point, Rowell Cove, shoreline trail openings
5

🌿 West Shirley Bog Specialist

~25–30 min from resort · Quiet dirt roads south of Greenville

An obscure local recommendation from Maine wildlife writers. Best from a canoe at daybreak, but moose are also seen from the CCC Road. Best for small-in-frame wildlife in vast wetland habitat.

GPS: ~45.35946, -69.67634
Access: Dirt-road approach, less formal infrastructure
Note: Higher confidence by canoe/kayak, lower by car
Composition: Wide wetland atmosphere, mist, distant mountain layering

📸 Camera Settings & Field Craft

🔭

Primary Lens

100–500mm or 150–600mm super-telephoto zoom. Ethics require distance — longer is better. Pair with a 24–70mm for landscape sessions.

⚡

Shutter Speed

1/500s+ for standing/feeding moose. 1/1000s+ if walking, crossing road, or shaking water. Don't sacrifice speed for low ISO!

📐

Aperture

Wide open to f/7.1 on super-tele zooms. Open wider for isolation; stop down slightly for moose-in-landscape compositions.

🎯

Autofocus

Continuous AF + Subject Tracking (animal detection if available). High-speed burst. Watch the histogram near bright water/sky.

🛋️

Support

A window-mounted beanbag is more useful than a tripod here — most encounters happen from the car or just outside it along roadsides.

🔋

Essentials

Extra batteries (cold mornings drain fast), large memory cards, lens cloth for fog/mist, and Auto ISO enabled.

⚠️ Safety, Ethics & Weather

!

Cows with Calves — Extra Distance

Maine IFW says if wildlife reacts to your presence, you are too close. Cows give birth in May — by your dates, calves are very young. Give them extra room. Never block escape routes.

🪰

Black Flies Are Brutal

Late April through June is peak season. Bring a head net, long sleeves, lightweight gloves, and DEET repellent — treat them as essential camera gear, not optional.

🚛

Logging Truck Right-of-Way

On dirt roads beyond Kokadjo: trucks have the right of way. Keep right, use lights, don't stop in the road, never block gates or side roads.

🌧️

Weather Contingency Plan

Clear dawn: Lazy Tom → Kokadjo. Fog/low cloud: Kokadjo + Lily Bay for atmosphere. Light rain: Prong Pond or Lily Bay. Heavy rain: Paved Lily Bay Road corridor only, stop-and-go from vehicle.

☁️

Overcast = Better Moose Photos

Don't cancel for clouds! Overcast or misty light lowers contrast and preserves detail on dark moose fur — often better than harsh sun for wildlife portraits.

🔇

Stay Quiet

Don't slam car doors. Keep your presence minimal. Never feed wildlife. Use your vehicle as a blind — moose are often more tolerant of a parked car than a person on foot.
